New to NJIT? Common Questions Answered by Students
Greek Life / Clubs:

Rush events happen during the first weeks of semester — attend the club fair (usually Week 2, Wednesday during common hours) to find fraternities and clubs
Club meetings typically happen during common hours: Wednesdays 2:30–3:55pm and Fridays 11:30am–12:55pm; some meet at night
Joining clubs is highly recommended — great way to meet people and you can use club offices to study when the library gets crowded
To find on-campus jobs, check njit.jobs or visit departments directly and ask

Academics:

Use Rate My Professors — upperclassmen recommend never picking a professor below 2.5
Ask upperclassmen for professor recommendations, especially for required courses
Calc 1 has roughly a 50% fail rate — many students retake it; don't be discouraged
Attendance is typically mandatory in humanities courses since class sizes are small
Class rank is not published until graduation and isn't very useful even then

Campus Life:

The game room in the Campus Center basement has bowling, ping pong, and pool tables
The main gym has free weights and squat racks but is older and can get crowded; the honors gym is newer but machines only
Don't get a meal plan as a commuter — students widely call it a ripoff; food trucks are a better option

Commuting by NJ Transit:

Sign up for the NJ Transit student pass through Banner (25% cheaper than standard monthly); must sign up by the 10th of the month for the next month's pass
Newark Light Rail is free if you already have a train pass (counts as a 1-zone bus)
Rutgers-Newark/NJIT run shuttle buses from Newark Penn Station to campus
Give yourself a 20–30 minute buffer between arriving and class; on exam days, give yourself an hour or more
NJ Transit delays are common — plan accordingly

Safety:

University Heights and downtown Newark are generally fine during the day
Avoid going off campus alone after 9–10pm
Use the campus shuttle to Penn Station rather than walking at night

Housing near NJIT (for grad students/off-campus):

Society Hill in Newark (Warren St area) — 24hr private security, $650–$900/month
Harrison and Kearny are popular with international grad students
