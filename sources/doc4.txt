Is NJIT Worth $20k/Year in Debt?
General consensus: Consider community college first to complete Gen Ed requirements — it can save you $35k+ in debt. Transfer credits to NJIT afterward.
On debt:

$80k total debt over 4 years is a lot — monthly payments will hit hard after graduation
One commenter graduated with only $10k in loans by doing CC first and using NJ Transit instead of dorming
If you're an NJ resident, commuting via NJ Transit (trains and buses go near campus) saves a significant amount

On NJIT's programs:

FinTech and Business & Information Systems (B&IS) are strong programs with high earning potential
NJIT has a solid combined business and computing school
A MechE grad reported starting at $120k within 3 years of graduating
Hiring managers reportedly prefer NJIT grads because "they know their stuff"

On housing:

NJIT dorms cost $5,000+ per semester
University Centre (right next to NJIT) offers rooms for ~$800/month — roughly half the price — and doesn't require a mandatory meal plan

On career success:

Get involved, do extracurriculars, get solid grades
Build your network early, attend networking events
Practice coding beyond coursework without relying on AI tools like Copilot/GPT
Internships are key — one person landed a cybersecurity job in banking through networking events and internships
