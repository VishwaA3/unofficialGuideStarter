Parking at NJIT as a Commuter — Tips from Students

Official parking pass ($325/semester):
Most students recommend just buying it — parking in Newark is generally expensive ($250/month at private lots), so the NJIT pass is actually subsidized
Even with a pass, parking is tough — Thursdays especially. Arrive before 7:30–8am to guarantee a spot
Evening classes (6pm+) are easier — spots open up by then
NJIT offers day passes if you only come 2–3 days a week: njit.edu/parking/daily-parking-options-students-employees
You get about a week after school starts before parking tags are enforced — use that time to test your options before committing

Free street parking near campus:
Everything west of Lock St is free (except Central St)
Sussex/Nesbitt is free past Summit St
Boyden Street — close to campus, free, students report no issues
Central Ave (near the Dunkin) has no meters — students parked there for 2+ years without issues
Avoid James St — cars have been sideswiped there

NJ Transit alternative:
NJ Transit offers a student pass 25% cheaper than standard monthly, includes local trips
If commuting only 2 days/week, single tickets are cheaper than a monthly pass
Worth calculating your actual driving cost (60 cents/mile + tolls + parking) vs. transit before deciding

General advice:
Skimping on parking to save money can backfire — tickets and towing add up fast; one commuter racked up 4 tickets and a tow before finally buying the pass
Schedule morning or evening classes to avoid the worst parking crunch (10am–8pm is brutal)
