Tips for Incoming Freshmen at NJIT (Commuter-Focused)

Food and health:
Do not rely exclusively on food trucks, Taco Bell, or cheap on-campus options — many students gain significant weight doing this
If commuting, bring food from home or explore healthier options around Rutgers-Newark and downtown Newark
The area around Rutgers-Newark and downtown has many good food options and is safe to explore during the day; avoid these areas at night

Getting involved:
CS majors: join the ACM — they offer tutoring for CS100, CS113, and CS114, and it is a great place to meet people and build connections
Computer Engineering and Electrical Engineering majors: join IEEE
For all other majors: find the club associated with your major
Clubs are also a great way to learn which professors are good and which to avoid
Commuters especially: do not go straight home after class — come early, attend club meetings, and use your free time on campus

Attending class:
Actually attend class — professors can drop you after 2–3 absences depending on the course
If you need to miss a class, go to the professor's office hours and find out what you missed
You are paying to be there, so make the most of it

Administrative tips:
Stay on top of bills, holds, and deadlines — waiting until the last minute can cause serious problems
Always check your account status proactively

Getting help:
If you are struggling in a class, seek help immediately — tutoring is available for math and most subjects
The Writing Center is very helpful for essays — students have gone from expecting a D to earning a B after a session
Highly recommended professor for humanities: Jon Curley (commutes from Manhattan, excellent teacher)

Choosing professors:
Avoid professors with below a 2.5 rating on RateMyProfessors
Plan your courses in advance using NJIT.xyz and always have backup course plans
Register for classes at midnight when registration opens — it is very competitive and waiting will result in a worse schedule

Social life:
NJIT is approximately 70% male — meeting people outside of campus (concerts, PATH to NYC, events near Prudential Center) is recommended
The PATH train to NYC is $2.75 and only about 15 minutes away
Commuters: arrive early and stay later — use free time to socialize and attend club events, not just to go to class and leave

Clubs and organizations:
Getting involved in campus clubs and organizations is highly recommended — most have GPA requirements which help keep you academically focused
Leadership roles in clubs are available and look great on a resume
