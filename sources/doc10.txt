NSO 2.0 at NJIT — What to Actually Expect

What happens during NSO 2.0:
First half of the day: meet people from your major (likely your FYS class too), scavenger hunt with your group leader
Second half: community service/volunteer work — past students have done food bank shifts, classroom setup, garden cleanup, decorating school hallways
Free food and a dinner to meet fellow students
Overall vibe: more of a bonding/icebreaker day than an information session — no real new information is given

Attendance:
Attendance is tracked repeatedly throughout the day via the Corq app and physical sign-in sheets — you can't just show up and leave
It is considered mandatory; some orientation leaders have said federal requirements are involved
Some students have skipped with no consequences; others advise emailing ahead saying you have a prior commitment
NJIT is not great at communicating NSO details ahead of time — check your NJIT email and the new student checklist at njit.edu/orientation/NSOchecklist

Honest student takes:
"Such a waste of time" — but good for meeting your future classmates
The volunteer work portion is widely criticized for being random and unengaging
The morning half is generally considered fine and low-pressure
